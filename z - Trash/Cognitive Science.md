**Behaviorist psychology**
	- has focused on ==observable stimuli and observable behavioral responses== and dismissed all talk of consciousness and mental representations.
**Cognitive science**
	-  has focused on ==common processes== underlying intelligent problem solving in computers and human brains.
**Evolutionary psychology**
	- has focused on ==particular cognitive mechanisms== adapted to particular ecological contexts.
**Dynamical systems**
	- has focused on ==how complexity can emerge== from a few variables interacting in accord with a few basic rules.

The three levels at which any machine carrying out an information-processing task can be understood
**Computational Theory**
	What is the goal of the computation, why is it appropriate, and what is the logic of the strategy by which it can be carried out?
**Representation and algorithm**
	How can this computational theory be implemented? In particular, what is the representation for the input and output, and what is the algorithm for the transformation?
**Hardware implementation**
	How can the representation and algorithm be realized physically

**Nativism, empiricism, and the origins of knowledge**
- Scholars are often labeled "nativists" or "empiricists" according to the kinds of answers that they provide to the previous questions
**Nativism**
	- knowledge is due to ==intrinsic properties of humans== perceptual and cognitive systems.
**Empericism**
	- knowledge is due to ==experience-based learning== form the environment.
- A main point in this article by Spelke is that these views, which are often considered diametrically opposed, may be more productively considered extreme end-points on a continuum where knowledge is "more-or-less" shaped by encounters with objects.
